## 解决方案：研究 Skill Graph

### 什么是研究 Skill Graph

**技术定义**: 一个互联 Markdown 文件夹，每个文件是一个"知识节点"，用 `[[wikilinks]]`（双括号链接）引用其他节点。

**比喻**: 不是问"研究主题 X"，而是强迫 AI 通过 6 个完全不同角度重新思考同一个问题。

```markdown
# Research Skill Graph — Command Center

## 1. Mission

Deep research system that takes ONE question and produces a multi-angle
analysis no single Google search or ChatGPT prompt could ever match.

Research Question: [PASTE YOUR QUESTION HERE]
Scope: [DEFINE BOUNDARIES]
Time Horizon: [how far back and forward]
Output Goal: [what decision does this inform?]

## 2. Node Map

### Methodology
- [[research-frameworks]] — how to approach different questions
- [[source-evaluation]] — criteria for judging source trust
- [[synthesis-rules]] — how to combine findings without losing nuance
- [[contradiction-protocol]] — what to do when sources disagree

### Lenses (the core engine)
- [[technical]] — what do numbers say? data only
- [[economic]] — follow the money
- [[historical]] — what patterns repeat?
- [[geopolitical]] — global chessboard
- [[contrarian]] — what if consensus wrong?
- [[first-principles]] — rebuild from fundamental truths

### Outputs
- [[executive-summary]] — 500 words max
- [[deep-dive]] — full analysis by lens
- [[key-players]] — people/orgs/countries that matter
- [[open-questions]] — what we STILL don't know

### Knowledge Base
- [[concepts]] — key terms, definitions, mental models
- [[data-points]] — hard numbers with source attribution

## 3. Execution Instructions

When given a research question:

1. Read this file completely
2. Read [[research-frameworks]] to pick right approach
3. Read [[source-evaluation]] for evidence criteria
4. For EACH lens in order (technical→economic→historical→
   geopolitical→contrarian→first-principles):
   a. Read the lens file for its specific angle
   b. Research the topic THROUGH that lens only
   c. Record findings, sources, confidence level
   d. Note contradictions with previous lenses
5. Read [[contradiction-protocol]] — resolve or document
6. Read [[synthesis-rules]] — combine everything
7. Produce all 4 output files
8. Update [[concepts]] and [[data-points]]

CRITICAL RULE: each lens must RETHINK the question, not just
add more information. technical and contrarian should feel like
written by two researchers who disagree. that tension is where
insight lives.
```

### 2. research-frameworks.md（选择方法）

```markdown
# Research Frameworks

## Framework Selection

### Type 1: "Is X true?" (Verification)
- Start: [[technical]] → [[contrarian]] → [[historical]]
- Best for: fact-checking, debunking
- Example: "Is nuclear fusion viable by 2035?"

### Type 2: "Why is X happening?" (Causal Analysis)
- Start: [[historical]] → [[economic]] → [[technical]]
  → [[geopolitical]] → [[contrarian]]
- Best for: understanding trends
- Example: "Why are birth rates collapsing globally?"

### Type 3: "What happens if X?" (Scenario Planning)
- Start: [[first-principles]] → [[technical]] → [[economic]]
  → [[geopolitical]]
- Best for: forecasting, strategy
- Example: "What happens to Europe if birth rates stay below
  replacement for 30 years?"

### Type 4: "What should I do about X?" (Decision Support)
- Run all 6 lenses in parallel
- Rank by lens agreement (5/6 = high confidence)
- Best for: investment decisions, policy

## Research Depth Levels

### Level 1: Quick Scan (30 min)
- 3 lenses max
- Top 5 sources only
- Goal: directional understanding

### Level 2: Standard Research (2-3 hours)
- All 6 lenses
- 15-25 sources
- Cross-reference findings
- Goal: informed opinion backed by evidence

### Level 3: Deep Dive (1-2 days)
- All 6 lenses with sub-questions
- 50+ sources including primary data
- Contradiction resolution required
- Goal: publishable analysis
```

### 3. source-evaluation.md（信任层级）

```markdown
# Source Evaluation

## Source Tier System

### Tier 1: Primary Data (highest trust)
- Raw datasets (UN, World Bank, national statistics)
- Peer-reviewed studies with methodology visible
- Financial filings, government records
- USE FOR: [[data-points]], hard claims

### Tier 2: Expert Analysis
- Reports from domain-specific research institutions
- Books by recognized authorities
- Long-form investigative journalism with cited sources
- USE FOR: interpretation, causal claims

### Tier 3: Informed Commentary
- Expert blog posts and newsletters
- Quality podcasts with domain experts
- Think tank reports (check funding)
- USE FOR: angles, hypothesis generation

### Tier 4: General Media
- Major news outlets
- Wikipedia (overview only, never final claims)
- USE FOR: initial orientation only, verify upstream

### Tier 5: Social/Anecdotal (lowest trust)
- Twitter threads, Reddit posts
- Personal anecdotes
- USE FOR: signal detection only

## Red Flags (downgrade 1 tier)
- No cited sources or methodology
- Author has financial incentive in conclusion
- Cherry-picked time frames
- Conflates correlation with causation
- Uses emotional language instead of evidence
```

### 4. synthesis-rules.md（合成不扁平化）

```markdown
# Synthesis Rules

## The Synthesis Process

### Step 1: Lens Summary
One paragraph per lens:
- Main finding
- Confidence: high/medium/low
- What surprised you

### Step 2: Agreement Map
- 4+ lenses agree → high confidence
- 3 lenses agree → moderate confidence
- 1-2 lenses support → hypothesis only, flag uncertain

### Step 3: Tension Map
- technical says X but economic says Y → this IS the insight
- Don't resolve by picking winner
- Ask: "under what conditions is each correct?"

### Step 4: Second-Order Insights
Best findings come from COMBINING lenses:
"The technical data shows X, but economic reveals Y — which
means first-principles argument about Z might be dominant"

### Step 5: Confidence Calibration
For each finding:
- CLAIM: what you believe is true
- EVIDENCE: strongest supporting data
- CONFIDENCE: high/medium/low
- WHAT WOULD CHANGE MY MIND: evidence to reverse conclusion

## Anti-Patterns
- Confirmation bias
- Narrative fallacy
- Recency bias
- Authority bias
- Anchoring
```

### 5. contradiction-protocol.md（真正洞察所在）

```markdown
# Contradiction Protocol

## When Two Sources Disagree

### Step 1: Check basics
- Same thing? (geography, time period, definitions)
- Same data? (different interpretation?)
- Tier difference? (see [[source-evaluation]])

### Step 2: Find root of disagreement
- Different data → find more complete/recent
- Different interpretation → examine reasoning
- Different scope → both might be right in context
- Different timeframe → short vs long-term
- Different incentives → check funding

### Step 3: Document, don't resolve
"Source A argues X based on data. Source B argues Y based
on data. Disagreement stems from root cause. Under
conditions C1, A is right. Under conditions C2, B is right."

### Step 4: Upgrade to [[open-questions]] if unresolvable
"We don't know whether X or Y" IS valuable intelligence

## When Two Lenses Disagree
This is EXPECTED and HEALTHY. Different lenses SHOULD produce tension.

## Confidence Adjustment
- 2 agree, 1 disagrees → investigate disagreeing lens deeper
- All agree → be suspicious (confirmation bias)
- No lenses agree → question too broad, narrow scope
```

### 6-11. 6 个透镜文件

每个透镜文件遵循相同结构：核心问题/如何研究/输出格式/声音/连接。

#### technical.md（技术透镜）

```markdown
# Lens: Technical

Strip away opinions. What do numbers say? What mechanisms?

## Core Questions
1. What does DATA show? (not what people say about data)
2. What are measurable inputs/outputs?
3. What mechanisms drive the phenomenon?
4. What are hard constraints (physical, biological, mathematical)?
5. What metrics matter and how measured?
6. Where is data incomplete?

## How to Research
- Tier 1 sources ONLY
- Look for: time series, geographic comparisons, breakdowns
- Quantify everything: "declined by X% between Y and Z"
- Find base rates: what's "normal"? historical range?

## Output Format
- METRIC: [what measured]
- VALUE: [the number]
- SOURCE: [Tier 1/2 with date]
- TREND: [direction and rate]
- CAVEAT: [measurement limitations]

## Voice
Clinical. precise. no emotional language.
"fertility rate dropped from 2.1 to 1.6" not "collapsing"
```
