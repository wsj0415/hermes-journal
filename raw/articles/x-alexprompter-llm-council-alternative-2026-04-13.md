## LLM Council 演进史

### Karpathy 原始版本（2025 年 11 月）

**设置**:
```
1. 同时向 GPT/Claude/Gemini/Grok 发送相同问题
2. 每个模型独立响应
3. 每个模型匿名审查其他模型响应
4. 主席模型合成最终答案
```

**核心洞察**:
> **四个不同模型。不同训练数据。不同架构。不同盲点。**

**Karpathy 观察**:
- 模型一致称赞 GPT-5.1 最有洞察力
- 但他个人偏好 Gemini 的简洁输出
- **模型与人类不同意。人类与模型不同意。**
- **那张力是价值所在**

**结果**: GitHub 17K stars，VentureBeat 报道

---

### Ole Lehmann 重建版（Claude 内运行）

**设置**:
```
5 个"思维风格"作为子 agent：
- the Contrarian（反对者）
- the First Principles Thinker（第一性原理思考者）
- the Expansionist（扩展者）
- the Outsider（局外人）
- the Executor（执行者）

每个顾问响应 → 匿名同行审查 → 主席合成
```

**优势**:
- ✅ 通过角色提示产生有用多样视角
- ✅ 快速，任何 Claude 订阅可用
- ✅ 可自定义分析透镜

**劣势**:
- ❌ 模型多样性消失（四不同模型 → 一模型扮演五角色）
- ❌ 同行审查受自我偏好偏差影响

## 三层增强方案

### Layer 1: 多模型多样性

> **Karpathy 的 Council 强大但固定。你无法改变分析框架。**

**Perplexity Computer 的 Model Council**:
```
1. 同时路由到三个前沿模型（GPT-5.4/Claude Opus 4.6/Gemini 3.1 Pro）
2. 每个独立响应
3. 第四合成器分析收敛点/分歧点/独特贡献
```

**价值**: **真实模型多样性而非模拟多样性**

---

### Layer 2: 自定义分析透镜

> **Ole 的 Council 让你定义模型如何思考。那真正有价值。**

**自定义 Skill 透镜示例**:

| 角色 | 透镜 |
|------|------|
| **投资者** | Bull Case / Bear Case / Macro Factors / Portfolio Fit |
| **创始人** | Customer Demand / Technical Feasibility / Market Timing / Competitive Edge |
| **创作者** | Audience Fit / Distribution Strategy / Monetization Path / Longevity Test |

**价值**: **Skill 只是 Markdown 文件。你写匹配你决策类型的透镜。**

---

### Layer 3: 言语化采样（Stanford 研究）

> **这部分真正让我惊讶。**

**问题**:
- 对齐训练后（RLHF/DPO），LLM 遭受**模式坍塌**
- 收敛于安全/典型/"最可能"响应
- 创造性/不寻常/高价值洞察被抑制因为模型学到熟悉=偏好
- **你问任何 AI 分析时，得到的是最典型分析，非最深刻分析**

**Stanford 解决方案**（Jiayi Zhang, Christopher Manning 等）:
```
1. 让模型生成多个响应 AND 分配概率分数
2. 明确告诉它从尾部采样 — 概率<0.10 的响应
3. 结果：1.6-2.1x 多样性提升（创意任务）同时保持质量
```

**关键特性**:
- 无需训练
- 适用于任何模型
- 与温度正交（可叠加，非替代）

---

### 三层协同效应

> **没人组合所有三层。这是我运行的栈。**

| 层级 | 多样性类型 | 来源 |
|------|-----------|------|
| **Layer 1** | 模型间多样性 | 不同训练数据/知识 |
| **Layer 2** | 模型内多样性 | 言语化采样解锁抑制洞察 |
| **Layer 3** | 分析多样性 | 自定义透镜聚焦你的决策类型 |

**结果**: **三层多样性同时工作**

---

### Skill 代码

```markdown
# Stress Test

You are a structured decision analysis system. When the user says 
"stress test this", "pressure test", "test this decision", or 
presents a choice between options, run the full protocol.

## Phase 1: Diverse perspective generation (with Verbalized Sampling)

For each analytical perspective, generate 3 candidate responses with 
estimated probability scores. Select the response with the LOWEST 
probability (the tail of the distribution) as the primary perspective. 
This ensures each perspective surfaces non-obvious, suppressed insights 
rather than the most typical analysis.

Use different models if available (Model Council). If single model, 
make perspectives maximally different: one quantitative, one strategic, 
one risk-focused, one unconventional.

Per perspective:
- state recommendation clearly (not "it depends")
- provide single strongest evidence
- flag what this perspective is NOT considering
- prioritize the non-obvious angle over the expected one

## Phase 2: Customizable analysis lenses

Default lenses (users: replace these with your own domain-specific lenses):

**Risk scan**: most likely failure mode? most expensive failure mode? same or different?

**Opportunity map**: adjacent upside user isn't seeing? what if this works 3x better? what would a competitor do if they knew?

**Execution audit**: fastest path to test with real data? actual bottleneck? minimum viable test?

**Assumption check**: what is user assuming without stating? what changes if wrong? are they solving the right problem?

## Phase 3: Decision brief

THE QUESTION: [restate — reframe if wrong question]

WHERE PERSPECTIVES AGREE: [2-3 convergence points]

WHERE PERSPECTIVES DISAGREE: [tensions with both sides' reasoning — highlight any tail-distribution insights that challenge the consensus]

RISK: [failure mode, one sentence]

BLIND SPOT: [unquestioned assumption, one sentence]

OPPORTUNITY: [unseen upside, one sentence]

VERDICT: [clear recommendation, 2-3 sentences — not "it depends"]

TEST IT THIS WEEK: [specific action + metric + threshold]

Under 500 words. No process explanation. Verdict must be real. 
Prioritize surprising, non-obvious findings over expected analysis.
```

## 实战案例：时事通讯付费墙决策

### 决策问题

> **Should I gate my newsletter behind a paid tier or keep it free with sponsorships?**
> （我应该将时事通讯设为付费还是保持免费带赞助？）

**背景**: 99K 订阅者

---

### 模型响应

**WHERE PERSPECTIVES AGREE（共识点）**:
- 两种路径在 99K 订阅者时都可行
- 决定因素非收入模型 — 是优化增长还是每订阅者收入
- 三模型独立收敛

---

**WHERE PERSPECTIVES DISAGREE（分歧点）**:

| 模型 | 观点 |
|------|------|
| **GPT** | 赞助规模计算（$3-6 CPM × 99K）= $297-594/发送<br>付费层$10/月×3% 转化 = $29,700/月<br>**50x 差异** |
| **Claude** | 转化免费到付费需根本上不同内容<br>可能破坏时事通讯运作方式 |
| **Gemini** | 提出无人问的混合模式：<br>核心内容免费，月度深度付费 |

---

**尾部洞察**（来自言语化采样）:
> **一个低概率响应标记：在 99K 订阅者时，时事通讯本身可能非收入产品 — 它是信任资产。变现层应坐在时事讯 ABOVE（课程/咨询/社区）非内部（付费墙）。时事讯的工作是注意力和信任，非直接收入。**

---

### 完整决策简报

```
THE QUESTION: should I gate my newsletter behind a paid tier or 
keep it free with sponsorships?

WHERE PERSPECTIVES AGREE: both paths viable at 99K subscribers. 
the deciding factor isn't revenue model — it's whether I optimize 
for growth or revenue per subscriber. three models converged 
independently.

WHERE PERSPECTIVES DISAGREE: GPT calculated sponsorship at my 
scale ($3-6 CPM on 99K) = $297-594/send. paid tier at $10/month 
with 3% conversion = $29,700/month. 50x difference. Claude pushed 
back: converting free to paid requires fundamentally different 
content that could break what makes the newsletter work. Gemini 
proposed a hybrid nobody asked about: core content free, monthly 
deep-dive paid.

tail-distribution insight (from verbalized sampling): one 
low-probability response flagged that at 99K subscribers, the 
newsletter itself might not be the revenue product - it's the 
trust asset. the monetization layer should sit ABOVE the newsletter 
(courses, consulting, community) not inside it (paywalls). the 
newsletter's job is attention and trust, not direct revenue.

RISK: launching paid without testing conversion kills free growth 
AND produces insufficient paid revenue.

BLIND SPOT: framing as "free vs paid" when the real question 
might be "is the newsletter the product or the distribution channel?"

OPPORTUNITY: hybrid tests paid demand with zero risk to free base. 
but the tail insight suggests a third path: newsletter stays free 
forever, monetization happens through products the newsletter sells.

VERDICT: don't paywall the newsletter. run a 4-week experiment 
selling a $49 product to the list instead. if it converts, the 
newsletter is a distribution channel and should stay free. if it 
doesn't, then test paid tier as fallback.

TEST IT THIS WEEK: one product offer to the full list. measure 
revenue per subscriber. compare against sponsorship CPM. let the 
data decide the newsletter's role.
```

## 自我偏好偏差研究

### 三篇顶级论文

| 论文 |  venue | 发现 |
|------|-------|------|
| **Paper 1** | NeurIPS 2024 | "LLM Evaluators Recognize and Favor Their Own Generations"<br>LLM 给自己输出打分高于其他模型 — 即使人类认为质量相等 |
| **Paper 2** | ICLR 2025 | "Self-Preference Bias in LLM-as-a-Judge"<br>机制：模型偏好风格熟悉的输出（通过困惑度测量）<br>越接近自己风格 = 越高分 |
| **Paper 3** | arXiv 2026 年 4 月 | "Self-Preference Bias in Rubric-Based Evaluation"<br>即使完全客观标准偏差仍存在<br>评审员错误标记自己输出为正确的概率高 50% |

---

### 关键细微差别

> **这研究关于评估步骤。不证明单模型角色生成无用 — 让 Claude 作为反对者 vs 扩展者思考确实产生不同输出。**

> **差距在同行审查步骤。Claude 审查 Claude 的五个输出无法有意义区分因为都"感觉"同样熟悉。**

**学术推荐**: **用多个不同模型减少自我增强偏差**
